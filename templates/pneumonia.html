Understanding Pneumonia

  • Pneumonia: Lung infection where the air sacs fill with pus or fluid; can be caused by bacteria, viruses, or fungi. Requires medical attention.
  • Normal: No signs of pneumonia detected; X-ray appears normal. If symptoms persist, consult your doctor as other conditions may exist.
What is pneumonia?
A serious infection affecting the lungs, leading to chills, cough, chest pain, fever, and shortness of breath. Early detection and treatment lowers risk of complications.

Who is at risk?
Young children, elderly, and people with weakened immune systems are more vulnerable. Regular checkups and vaccinations reduce risk.
